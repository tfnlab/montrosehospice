Choose Your Service

Pain and symptom management

Pain and symptom management in hospice care aims to relieve discomfort and improve quality of life for individuals with a life-limiting illness. It includes physical symptom management and emotional/spiritual support provided by a team of healthcare professionals.

Call

Emotional and spiritual support

Emotional and spiritual support in hospice care addresses the non-physical needs of individuals facing a life-limiting illness. This support helps individuals and their loved ones cope with the emotional and spiritual aspects of the end-of-life journey, including grief, anxiety, depression, and spiritual questions.

Call

Medical equipment and supplies

Medical equipment and supplies are provided in hospice care to support the comfort and well-being of individuals facing a life-limiting illness. This can include items such as hospital beds, oxygen equipment, wheelchairs, and other durable medical equipment necessary to manage symptoms and maintain comfort at home.

Call

Medications related to the terminal illness

Medications related to the terminal illness are provided in hospice care to manage symptoms and improve quality of life for individuals facing a life-limiting illness. These medications may include pain management medications, medications to manage other symptoms such as nausea or constipation, and medications to address emotional symptoms like anxiety or depression.

Call

Nursing care

Nursing care in hospice care involves ongoing assessment and management of the individual's physical, emotional, and spiritual needs by trained nursing professionals. It includes administration of medications, wound care, and other medical interventions as needed to maintain comfort and dignity.

Call

Social services

Social services in hospice care provide support and resources to individuals and their families facing a life-limiting illness. This may include assistance with practical tasks, such as financial and legal planning, as well as emotional support, counseling, and connection to community resources.

Call

Dietary counseling

Dietary counseling in hospice care provides guidance on nutrition and hydration to individuals facing a life-limiting illness. This can include recommendations on healthy eating habits, meal planning, and tips for managing changes in appetite or digestion. The goal is to help individuals maintain their strength and comfort, and to improve quality of life.

Call

Therapeutic and support groups

Therapeutic and support groups in hospice care offer individuals and their families facing a life-limiting illness an opportunity to connect with others who are also on a similar journey. These groups may provide emotional support, grief counseling, and a safe space to share experiences and feelings. They can also offer practical information and resources to help cope with the challenges of a life-limiting illness.

Call

Volunteer services

Volunteer services in hospice care provide support to individuals and their families facing a life-limiting illness. These volunteers may offer practical help, such as running errands or providing transportation, as well as emotional support, such as companionship and listening. The goal is to reduce stress and improve quality of life for both the individual and their family.

Call

Bereavement support for families

Bereavement support for families in hospice care provides ongoing assistance to the loved ones of individuals who have passed away. This support may include grief counseling, support groups, and connection to community resources. The goal is to help families cope with the loss of their loved one and to provide a safe and supportive environment as they navigate their grief.

Call

Respite care

Respite care in hospice care provides temporary relief for primary caregivers. This care can take place in the individual's home or in a hospice facility, and can last from a few hours to several days. The goal of respite care is to give the primary caregiver a break, allowing them to rest, recharge, and attend to personal needs, while ensuring that their loved one continues to receive high-quality care and support.

Montrose Hospice Offers Compassionate Care for Those Facing Terminal Illness

Montrose Hospice provides a range of services to help patients and their families through the end-of-life journey with dignity, comfort, and support.

Montrose Hospice is a comprehensive hospice care provider located in the heart of the city. Their dedicated team of professionals includes doctors, nurses, social workers, and spiritual care advisors who work together to provide personalized care for individuals and families facing terminal illness.

The services offered at Montrose Hospice include pain and symptom management, emotional and spiritual support, in-home care, respite care, and bereavement support. The hospice's focus is on quality of life and ensuring that patients and their loved ones have the necessary resources to cope with the physical, emotional, and spiritual challenges that come with a terminal illness. Montrose Hospice's compassionate care and commitment to their patients have earned them a reputation as one of the top hospice providers in the area.
